Popularity and Subversion

Recently, John Lydon (good thing he doesn’t go by Rotten anymore because he doesn’t deserve it) said that being conservative was the new punk because punk was always the opposite of what was the going concensus or ‘what was popular’ and sense ‘woke’ is popular, the punk thing to do is to oppose that. This is an astonishingly moronic assertion from a guy that I assumed got it. Didn’t he have to get it? Wasn’t that the point? And not just with the Sex Pistols, but on to PIL and beyond? This guy got it, right? I’ve seen this before - in highschool. I came of age in the 80’s and 90’s and gravitated toward the punk subculture - whatever that may mean - once I got to highschool. Before the grunge scene broke, the alternative scene was incredibly heterogenous. You saw the same people at a They Might Be Giants show that you saw at a Cure show that you saw at a Rancid show - more or less. I have written extensively on this elsewhere. The alternative scene was perfectly by Perry Ferrell’s first Lollapalooza. That lineup defined the mentality of all the little subculture niches of alternative on one stage: it was about being genuine and not being merely a commodity. It was all DIY and it was fringe. As a result, it was easy to differentiate ourselves from the Soc’s: they listened to pop music and shopped at Dillards; we listened to community radio shows on certain days at certain times and we shopped at Crash-n-Burn. When all the events of the mid-nineties combined and the results became apparent - Ministry stickers on Jocks’ pickup trucks, Doc Martins at Dillards, Green Day on the top 40 - we had to intellectualize what we were about without the popularity heuristic. And we did. Well, a lot of us did. A lot of us grew up but some of us committed to the bit. We abhorred the sell-out in principle, but we understood that it had become a monster that artists had no control over. Formerly, we had been our own gatekeepers. We made mixes for our square friends that introduced them to some cool shit but we chose those people and gave them a chance to have a little taste of what we were doing. Sometimes they wanted to shift gears and change their scene; most just thought it gave them a little street cred to know a few things about x, y, or z and that was fine too. Now there was no gate to be kept. Record companies want to sell records, they kind of have to. That doesn’t justify the gross exploitation of artists but it does explain why we couldn’t count on them to hold the line. And the bands have no control once they’ve signed off and if you’re in a band and someone wants to make it so you can be a full time musician and not washing dishes at Perkins, you’re gonna take it unless you really have some spine. Ani Difranco held her ground and Righteous Babe survives untouched. Most of the other labels that our DIY heroes started got subsumed just like the music festivals. ...

Freedom & Liberty

These terms seem to be used interchangeably quite often when they, in fact, have an inverse relationship. Liberty is your God-give right to self-actualize, to walk the Earth in search of adventure and enlightenment. If that’s not what you’re doing with it, I can’t help you. Freedom, on the other hand, is a gift from your fellow travelers that helps you maintain your liberty. Often this other is the state but in the long run and at it’s base it is a the gift we give each other whether it is part of a social contract to be a civilized, well-ordered people, whether it is in my self-interest to reciprocate such in principle, or whether I am simply indifferent to you. God gave you the tools to exploit the successes of those who reared and educated you and to recover from their mistakes. God gave you the ability to think critically and to conduct yourself in manner that will encourage your success whether it is as a member of that civilized, well-ordered group, whether you realize your success is contingent upon others’, or whether it’s by minding your own business. For those already hung up on the use of God so far, it is merely a shorthand that reflects the attitude of the author. The words above survive the replacement of God with whatever source of life or consciousness you ascribe to. Ulitmately, Liberty is innate to our being. It is Liberty that compels me to write this and it is freedom I tacitly request from you to allow me to express my interpretation of the world, the freedom I afford you to use it metaphorically to fit the way you interpret the world and it is something more interesting than either of those concepts that motivates us to understand one another - each in a language that makes most sense to us. That should be enough on the topic. From this premise anyone should be able to move forward and just do their best. But we are a species incredibly bound to language and words, often for no better reason than habit or self-interest. It turns out when there’s a few hundred million of us, statistically it is incredibly unlikely that we all make the same deductions, prioritize the same values, or use the same measure of success in the process of self-actualization. As was noted, freedom is a gift and no one is entitled to it. You can make all the rules and enforce all the laws you want but nothing other than intentional action is going to keep me from putting a brick through your skull if I deem it a sensible solution to the obstruction of my understanding of self-actualization. There may be well-advertised consequences for doing so but there are two important characteristics of consequences: a) they occur after the act has been committed and b) they are only meaningfully evaluated by their target. The consequences of any action are evaluated in some quantitative measure as is the conceptual resolution of my dilemma. The mechanisms of the black box that breaks this down to an arithmetic problem are complex but somehow a “yea” or “nay” is reached. More often than not, people aren’t getting smashed in the head with a brick at the end of each of these encounters but anything with a non-zero probability of occurring will, in fact, happen eventually. I have tried to keep this from being a dated piece but I feel that some specific language as it is being used contemporarily is necessary to make my point. I am writing this in the US in the wake of the 2024 Presidential Election. It is an emotional time for a lot of people if for no other reason than over the last decade, Americans and much of the Western world has been convinced that politics is the most salient feature in their pursuit of self-actualization. The veracity of that conclusion is dubious but it does seem that the stakes are higher than they used to be. I’m not certain that’s the case, but regrddless it seems to be the prevailing attitude, at least in the West. What is being given center-stage is typified by the term “woke.” Itself, this term is problematic in that is has been co-opted by all to nearly comic effect. To any American English speaker, it should be plainly obvious that intentional mis-conjugation is a feature of African American Vernacular Slang. My choices here to describe this term are that absurd mouthful of an academic term or simply saying “how black folks talk” and both raise feathers. Neither is meant pejoratively and I’m just trying to get to the meat of this. We all know that American black culture (and other cultures) have their own way of communicating colloquially and in a lot of cases and in a lot of variations the results can be musical, hilarious, dramatic or simply effective. This is why we pay Sam Jackson a million dollars everytime he says “mother fucker” and we don’t pay any white dudes that much money for articulating a hard-R “mother fucker.” Once in awhile we might (See “Die Hard” - 1988) but not as a discernable pay-scale. And we know that it isn’t a spelling distinction because Jules’ wallet says “Bad Mother Fucker.” So “woke?” Even if we conjugate it to “Having Been Awakened” that sentiment reflects a way of speaking about enlightenment that is cultural - more closely related to black Christian Evangelical phrasing. It isn’t a very white sentiment to allude to a state of being arrived at as though having been metaphorically asleep and thus unaffected or otherwise unmoved by the happenings that have been going on while you slept cozy in your room. Having risen and drawn back the curtains, we see the world in tatters and we should feel some sense of urgency in reclaiming and re-civilizing this apocalyptic Hell-scape. I mean that phrasing was incredibly white, but the sentiment “oh so now you awake” is less so. To go a bit further, as an awakening, if my origin story here is at all accurate, it was probably intended to refer to white people realizing that there is overwhelming evidence of sustained systemic abuse in the form of violence on the part of the law enforcement community aimed at black Americans. So white people stop fucking saying “woke.” We were supposed to internalize it, not coopt it. Now It has lost all meaning and we had no business talking like that in the first place. But the sentiment remains. Unfortunately, it encompasses a whole host of largely liberal values and they all seem to have been given equal footing. This is a mistake that the Democrats have consistently made my entire life. That we have put on equal footing an intolerance for murdering people during a traffic stop and the transgression of not referring to you in the third-person buy the preferred term that you that adheres to know rules of language but is rather determined by you alone and must be explicitly reported to others, is insane. In all likelihood, nobody is interested in you and is certainly not having lengthy discussions about you later. If your existential intent was to be of such a spectacle that we would more than likely want to talk about you later, then that’s incredibly sad. Otherwise, the grammarian motivation behind pronouns was meant to simpliy speech, not complicate it. I don’t think I’m alone in feeling like my life has a soundtrack, a lot of which is out of my control. As much as I try to curate the sounds I am surrounded by, a lot of stuff that I wouldn’t choose gets through and finds a salience in my little brain that triggers nearly a nearly disembodying sense of place and time. As much as I know that I don’t want to be a Dave Matthews fan, you couldn’t walk across my college campus in the Fall of ‘94 without hearing “Ants Marching” echoing out the windows of nearly every dorm you passed. He came up again on a night home from school a few years later with the release of their next album. My best friend from highschool, Dave, was in town, and we were driving to a little get-together that would be a memorable night. Dave had a habit of getting sucked into certain albums and it was infectious the way he made you believe it was the best thing around. This happened to us my senior year. Counting Crows “August and Everything After” is a funny one. First, he was obsessed with it but it was also one of the albums in the 5-disc changer at a restaurant I worked at. It usually came on early in the evening but I mostly remember hearing “‘Round Here” late at night on its second rotation while I was stacking chairs and cleaning up. It was a bummer of a song and gelled with my feeling that I was going nowhere. I was supposed to head off to the aforementioned college in the Fall, but my grades my last semester were less than stellar. I got a letter from them in the mail asking me to offer an explaination. I never responded. I didn’t know how, or I was overwhelmed with fear, or whatever but I didn’t so I assumed I was going to get a subsequent letter telling me to fuck off and I was going to spend the rest of my life bussing tables at Bobby’s Bistro. Meanwhile, Dave would blare “Mr. Jones” and rock out in the car and we’d have a blast. He did this with Green Day’s “Dookie” as well. You’ll notiec this were all very popular albums. It really ruined his street cred but it was infectious the way he made you believe him. He’d do it in subsequent years with other albums and he actually got me and my now wife to come out to Coachella the year of the indie bands.
