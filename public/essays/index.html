Nobody Move, Nobody Gets Hurt

This is everyone’s favorite part of the bank job. Our guys- we’re usually rooting for the robbers - are letting everyone know that they have no intention of committing wanton violence; they’re here for the bank’s money, not your money. You have no reason to be a hero, it will gain you nothing and we’ll be forced to maintain control by the means we have at our disposal. People will get hurt, it will be your fault and you’ll be right back in the same situation. Unfortunately, this is the least likely scenario that any of us will encounter where this sort of exchange takes place. I would love to watch a gang of tactically competent, smooth ass criminals walk out of a bank with untold riches. What is much more likely is that this is the tacit warning you will receive from an adversary, an abuser, or an oppressor. Behave. Don’t make me hurt you. Don’t force me to respond. Do not make waves. Do what you’re told or there will be consequences. How are we to receive this message? Do we trust that this person, these people, this institution has only our best interest in mind and wants to protect us from our poor understanding of things? Are we perhaps not privvy to certain knowledge that justifies the need for subserviance in the name of the greater good? The answer is always, without exception, “no.” This statement or its implication reveals fear - the fear that the recipient becomes aware of how precarious this apparent power imbalance is.
