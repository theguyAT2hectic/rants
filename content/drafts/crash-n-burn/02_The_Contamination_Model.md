Kenny G and Docs at Macy's as parallel mechanisms
The sax guy archetype — Tim Cappello, Clarence Clemons, the cultural weight of the instrument
The sax as uniquely cool because it transferred decades of jazz mythology instantly and required no athletic performance the way guitar heroism did
Duotones 1986 — five million records, slow contamination begins
Breathless 1992 — twelve million records, total name brand capture of the instrument
The soprano saxophone specifically dragging down the entire family
The aesthetic without the ethos — you can buy the signifier but the meaning doesn't transfer
Docs at Macy's — the object unchanged, the meaning completely evacuated
The friction of finding the thing was how the values got transmitted. Remove the friction and you have a boot with no soul
